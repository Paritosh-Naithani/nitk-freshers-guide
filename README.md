# NITK Fresher's Guide

A visual novel designed to help students navigate college life through stories, conversations, mistakes, experiences, and advice.

## About The Project

NITK Fresher's Guide started as a simple idea:

"What if the advice that seniors give to freshers could be presented as a visual novel instead of a long PDF or a boring document?"

The result is an interactive visual novel where a fresher arrives on campus and learns about academics, hostels, friendships, clubs, opportunities, mistakes, and college life through conversations with a senior.

Although the game is set in NIT Karnataka (NITK), the advice and lessons are intended to be useful for students from any college.

The primary goal of this project is not entertainment alone. It is to help students avoid common mistakes, make better decisions, and get the most out of their college years.

---

## Inspiration

This project draws inspiration from several sources.

The visual novel format and presentation style were heavily inspired by the Ace Attorney series, one of my favorite game franchises. The dialogue-driven storytelling, character interactions, and visual presentation encouraged me to experiment with the visual novel format.

The title "NITK Fresher's Guide" was loosely inspired by the naming style of *A Good Girl's Guide to Murder*. I liked how the title immediately tells the audience what they are about to experience.

Despite these inspirations, the story, structure, dialogue flow, advice sections, menu design choices, and overall concept were entirely my own.

## Why I Made This

When students enter college, they are flooded with information.

Everyone talks about:

* CGPA
* Coding
* Placements
* Clubs
* Internships
* Friendships
* Research

But very few people explain how all of these things fit together.

As a student myself, I have seen many people make avoidable mistakes simply because nobody explained things properly.

This project attempts to bridge that gap.

Instead of presenting advice as a list of bullet points, it presents it through conversations and experiences that feel more natural and memorable.
